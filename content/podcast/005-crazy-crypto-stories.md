**Crazy Story #1**

You probably all have heard of a few cases where a million dollars in Bitcoin was lost or they throw out a hard drive and they could never find it again. And that is exactly what this story is about. In the case of James Howells, a 35-year-old IT engineer from Wales United Kingdom. He cleaned his house in 2013 and discarded by accident a hard drive with a lot more Bitcoin than he originally thought. He had two identical hard drives at the time and threw away the wrong one, aka the one that still contains the private keys for a wallet with 7500 BTC. James Howells offered to donate 25% of the Bitcoins found to a COVID relief on an effort to let this man dig through the city’s trash. And speaking of treasure, it actually leads us to an even crazier crypto story that we’re going to end today’s episode with.

**Crazy Story #2**

So, this story is about treasure left on Mount Everest. In May 2018, a question and answer social media company called ask.fm was planning to launch its own “Initial Coin Offering”, also known as an ICO to fund a new platform that worked with its native token ASKT [phonetic 06:12]. So they thought it would be a great idea to send a group of professional climbers to Mount Everest and bury on the top, a ledger hardware wallet containing 500,000 ASKT. This would be around $100,000, after the ICO and above all, a really awesome crazy crypto story that I’m telling you right now.

So they actually did it, they climbed to the top of Mount Everest and took out a ledger hardware wallet with 500,000 ASKT their native token and buried it at the top of Mount Everest so they recorded the event and challenge the general public to find and pick up this treasure by any means possible.

So a crazy marketing idea, crazy idea to add to your ICO and the best part is, that’s actually not the craziest part about this story, because it’s about to get a lot more wild. So after they record this event, put it on YouTube, and challenge the world to find this treasure on top of Mount Everest, no joke, nearly everything went wrong after that point.

The first thing that went wrong, went terribly wrong, was a Sherpa guide disappeared on the way back. Not only did the Sherpa disappear on the way back, a handful of the climbers got hurt, and they had to get rescued off of Mount Everest. The very sad part about this story is that the guide was actually never found and in the end, the ICO was never launched. So the coins are still probably up on the top of Mount Everest covered in a ton of snow and forgotten, just like **@ask.fm**.
